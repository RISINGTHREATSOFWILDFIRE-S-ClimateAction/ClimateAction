REDUCE: Preventing Wildfires in Bukidnon

Most wildfires in Bukidnon are human-caused — from unregulated agricultural burning to careless disposal of flammable materials. Reducing wildfire risks starts with simple, intentional actions that protect our forests, wildlife, and livelihoods while fighting climate change.

Since Bukidnon’s economy relies heavily on agriculture and eco-tourism, preventing fires is critical for both people and the planet. Here’s how we can all work together to reduce wildfire threats:

  • Avoid open burning for farming — use composting or no-till methods instead
  • Dispose of cigarette butts properly (never throw them in dry grass/forests)
  • Keep fire pits at least 10 meters away from trees and brush, and extinguish completely
  • Clear dry vegetation around homes and farms to create firebreaks
  • Report illegal burning or suspicious activity to local authorities immediately
  • Support reforestation projects to restore dry, fire-prone areas with native trees

Action Plans for Different Groups in Bukidnon

For Farmers

Adopt sustainable farming practices like "slash-and-mulch" instead of slash-and-burn. Join local training on organic composting to improve soil health without burning.

For Communities

Organize quarterly clean-ups to remove flammable debris from public areas. Set up neighborhood fire watch teams during dry seasons.

For Students

Start a school climate club to teach peers about wildfire prevention. Host poster contests to spread awareness in your barangay.
